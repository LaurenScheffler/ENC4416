This trending topic came to light right after Amazonâ€™s stock reached a new record, most likely due to the COVID-19 national quarantine occurring at the time. In response to the trending topic, some social media users were confused as the topic was on the rise. Many users on Twitter, however, spread #ripjeffbezos with images of people or characters that look like him or are bald and sentiments of seemingly kind remorse. The confused messages questioning the CEOâ€™s death did not gain much traction while those mocking his death with false imagery had thousands of likes and retweets. Following the initial post sparking this topic, one may assume that these subsequent rhetors were piggybacking off the joke already started. The only twist the Twitter atmosphere added to posts on the trending topic was the usage of images and videos that were not Jeff Bezos. Many of these images and videos were not even relating to Bezos which became the joke rather than the text itself. These behaviors, exhibited through social media posts, are similar to that of what Erika Sparby mentions. Sparby states, "memes act as cultural units that spread any idea from person to person...[t]hey influence behavior, not only on an individual level but also on a group, or collective, level" (86). Memes mentioned by Sparby relate to the actions of those who posted about Jeff Bezos because in a way each post is a response to one another, and the posts mimic each other by the similarity of their layouts.


When the topic first began, the anime community, who was a part of the group chat who birthed the idea of this trend, took part by posting tweets incorporating the hashtag while mainly posting media content from Avatar The Last Airbender. Due to a large amount of posting by this ingular community, which has many people within it fixating on the topic #ripjeffbezos, this activity and a large number of likes on posts helped spread the topic. With these factors in mind and persistent posting, the hashtag started to gain an audience rather quickly leading it to become a trending topic within 20 minutes holding that title into the next day. The negative opinions people hold for Bezos and the combination of his supposed death was a major appeal to the target audience of this trend. Many poked fun at Jeff Bezos, memorializing him with inaccurate images and videos helping spread this misinformation.
